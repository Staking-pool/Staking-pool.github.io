How it works

Use a Staking Pool in three clean steps

A pool lets multiple users participate in staking or reward programs without running every piece of infrastructure themselves. The app keeps the flow wallet-first, with live terms shown before each transaction.

Step 01

Connect your wallet

Open the app and connect through a standard wallet connection. You keep custody of your keys and review each request before approval.

Step 02

Choose an asset and pool

Select an eligible asset, compare the live pool route, and check withdrawal timing, reward mechanics, and any protocol conditions shown in the app.

Step 03

Stake and track

Confirm the transaction from your wallet, then monitor your pool position, claim status, or unstaking path from the dashboard as the network updates.

Why stake

A sharper way to evaluate pool rewards

Pool access without custody

Participate in pooled staking while the signing authority stays with your wallet. The app prepares the transaction; you decide whether to approve it.

Pool rewards, lockups, validator queues, and DeFi market conditions can move. Staking Pool keeps the current route inside the app before you commit.

FAQ

Staking Pool questions

01

What is a staking pool?

A staking pool groups user stake into a shared on-chain position or reward route. It helps users participate without operating validator infrastructure or managing every reward contract manually.

02

Is Staking Pool non-custodial?

Yes. The app is designed around self-custody: your wallet signs transactions, and you should verify the asset, contract, chain, and amount before approving.

03

How long does staking or unstaking take?

Timing depends on the asset, chain, validator queue, pool contract, and network confirmation. The dashboard shows the relevant status before and after you submit a transaction.

04

What does it cost?

You pay applicable network fees and any pool or protocol costs shown by the app before signing. Rates and costs are not fixed on this page.

05

What are the main risks?

Staking can involve smart contract risk, validator risk, slashing, liquidity changes, market volatility, and pool-specific rules. Compare live pool data with broader DeFi yield markets before acting.

06

Which chains are supported?

Supported chains and assets can change as pools are added or removed. See the full list on the supported chains page before connecting a wallet.

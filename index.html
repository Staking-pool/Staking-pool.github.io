What is Staking Pool?

Lido is an Ethereum liquid staking protocol that batches user deposits with validators and mints stETH as a transferable staking token. Review the Lido docs before connecting a wallet.

How Staking Pool works

  1. Open the appGo to stake.lido.fi and verify the domain before connecting any wallet.
  2. Connect walletUse a self-custody Ethereum wallet and confirm the selected network is Ethereum mainnet.
  3. Stake ETHEnter the ETH amount, review the transaction details, and submit only after checking risks and gas costs.
  4. Manage positionTrack stETH in your wallet, use the Lido app for rewards and withdrawals, and keep recovery phrases offline.

Before you stake

Liquid access

ETH submitted through Lido receives stETH, a transferable token designed to reflect a staked position while rewards and penalties depend on validator performance.

Transparent contracts

Core protocol addresses can be checked against the stETH contract on Etherscan before interacting.

Risk-aware routing

Rewards are variable, not guaranteed, and users should consider smart contract, validator, slashing, liquidity, and governance risks.

Staking Pool FAQ

What is a staking pool for ETH?

It is a pooled way to participate in Ethereum validation without running a solo validator yourself; pooled and liquid options are described in the Ethereum staking guide.

Is this staking pool the official Lido app?

No. This page routes users to the official Lido staking interface at stake.lido.fi; always verify the domain before connecting a wallet.

What token do I receive from the staking pool?

When staking ETH through Lido, users receive stETH on Ethereum, which is designed to represent staked ETH plus protocol-linked rewards and penalties.

Can I withdraw from the staking pool?

Lido provides a withdrawals flow in the official app, but timing and final amounts depend on Ethereum validator exits, protocol mechanics, and network conditions.
